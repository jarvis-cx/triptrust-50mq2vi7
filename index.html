Security & Safety

Low–Medium

Overall Crime Level

London is broadly safe, particularly in central business districts. Violent crime rates are low in areas typically visited by business travellers. The primary risk is opportunistic petty theft.

⚠️ UK Terrorism Threat: SUBSTANTIAL As of March 2026, the UK terrorism threat level is Substantial (3rd of 5 levels), meaning an attack is considered likely. The threat is under review following regional geopolitical developments. Remain vigilant in crowded public spaces, transport hubs, and iconic landmarks.

Top Safety Risks

  • 1
    Pickpocketing & Phone Theft Highest risk in crowded areas: Oxford Street, Westminster, Covent Garden, London Bridge. Phone snatching is on the rise — keep your device secured and off the table in restaurants.
  • 2
    Terrorism UK terror threat remains Substantial. Stay alert at transport hubs (Paddington, Waterloo, Victoria), crowded venues, and public events. Follow any official guidance immediately.
  • 3
    Scams & Overcharging Common tourist scams include unlicensed minicabs, fake charity collectors, and overpriced restaurant menus in tourist zones. Stick to licensed black cabs or Uber/Bolt.
  • 4
    Night Safety in Outer Areas Certain outer London areas (parts of Hackney, Lewisham, Peckham) have higher crime at night. Central business districts and hotel areas are safe.

Getting Around London

From Heathrow Airport (LHR)

✈️ Elizabeth Line Best option. Direct from Heathrow Terminals 2/3/4/5 to central London (Paddington, Bond Street, Liverpool Street). ~30–45 mins. ~£12–15. Runs every 10 mins. Contactless payment accepted.
🚄 Heathrow Express Premium express to Paddington Station. 15 mins. ~£25–37 (book in advance online for discounts). Business class available.
🚇 Piccadilly Line Budget option. Direct Tube service to central London. ~50–60 mins. ~£5–6 with Oyster/contactless. Runs 24/7 at weekends.
🚕 Black Cab / Minicab Door-to-door convenience. £50–80 to central London depending on traffic. Use licensed black cabs or pre-booked Uber/Bolt — never unlicensed minicabs outside the terminal.

From Gatwick Airport (LGW)

🚄 Gatwick Express Non-stop to Victoria Station. 30 mins. ~£18–22. Every 15–30 mins. Most convenient for Mayfair/West End hotels.
🚂 Southern / Thameslink Cheaper train services to London Bridge, Blackfriars, St Pancras. ~35–45 mins. ~£10–17.
🚕 Taxi ~45–90 mins depending on traffic. £80–120+. Not recommended unless essential — train is faster and cheaper.

Getting Around the City

  • London Underground (Tube) — Fast, reliable, comprehensive. Use contactless bank card or Oyster card. Daily/weekly price caps apply automatically.
  • Elizabeth Line (Crossrail) — Modern, spacious, excellent Wi-Fi. Connects Heathrow ↔ Paddington ↔ City ↔ Canary Wharf ↔ Stratford.
  • Black Cabs — Hail anywhere in central London or book via Gett app. Fully licensed, metered, safe 24/7.
  • Uber / Bolt — Widely available, competitive pricing. Always verify driver details before getting in.
  • Santander Cycles (Boris Bikes) — Docking stations everywhere in central London. £1.65/30 mins. Great for short daytime trips.
  • Walking — Most City and West End meetings are walkable. Distances often shorter than they appear on the Tube map.

Culture & Business Etiquette

ℹ️ You Know This Culture As a British national, you're operating in your home business culture. The below is a reminder of key norms — particularly useful if you're hosting international guests or attending formal City institutions.

Core Business Norms

  • Punctuality is paramount. Being late to a meeting is a serious faux pas. Arriving 5 minutes early is standard. If running late, always call ahead.
  • Formality first. Address people as Mr/Mrs/Ms until invited to use first names — particularly in the City, legal, and financial sectors.
  • Understatement and indirectness. "That's quite interesting" often means disagreement. Listen between the lines. Direct confrontation is considered impolite.
  • The firm handshake remains standard for first meetings. Eye contact is important. Excessive displays of enthusiasm can be read as insincere.
  • Dress code: City and financial services — formal (dark suit, tie for senior meetings). Tech/creative sectors — smart-casual is increasingly the norm. Check the sector before dressing down.

Business Dining

  • Business lunches are common in the City. The Ivy City Garden, Hawksmoor Guildhall, and Boisdale of Bishopsgate are City staples.
  • For Mayfair/West End dining: Scott's, Sexy Fish, and The Ritz Restaurant for special occasions.
  • The host pays at business meals. Do not split bills in client entertainment — always offer to pay as a sign of hospitality.
  • Wine is customary at business dinners. It's fine to drink moderately. Non-alcoholic options are completely accepted and increasingly common.
  • The "pub lunch" is a legitimate business meeting format for informal discussions.

London-Specific Tips

  • Stand on the right side of escalators — the left is for walking. Violating this is a serious social crime on the Tube.
  • Queue culture is real. Jumping a queue will be met with cold stares and passive-aggressive tutting.
  • Small talk: weather, football (know which team your counterpart follows), and light current events. Avoid politics and Brexit in business settings.
  • Private members clubs (The Ivy Club, Soho House, Annabel's) are common entertainment venues for senior client meetings — smart dress required.

General Social Rules

  • Alcohol is freely available but excessive public drunkenness is frowned upon — particularly in professional settings
  • Photography: generally fine in public. Avoid photographing police operations or military installations.
  • No restrictions on dress code in public (though dress for the weather and the meeting)
  • Mobile phones: acceptable in use but put away during face-to-face meetings — keep on silent
